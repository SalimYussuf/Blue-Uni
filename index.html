Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about admissions, fees, and campus life.

Stream A is designed for fresh graduates. You need a recognized Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering or a related field, foundational understanding of power systems theory, commitment to safety protocols, and a clear background check with medical fitness for high-voltage environments.

Program durations vary by module. Stream A modules range from 4 to 8 weeks, while Stream B CPD modules for in-service personnel typically run 3 to 4 weeks. The mandatory HV Safety module is a 1-week intensive for all participants.

Tuition varies by module, ranging from $310 to $465 for Stream A. Stream B modules are typically employer-sponsored. We offer merit-based bursaries, the Sompower Capacity Grant for underserved regions, and a Women in Power Engineering Scholarship. Fees can be paid in full (5% discount) or in 3 installments.

Yes! We offer Technician Lodging at $650/semester (twin-sharing with WiFi, cleaning, and cafeteria access) and Engineer Suites at $1,100/semester (single ensuite rooms with kitchenette and premium security). Both options are located on or near campus in Hargeisa.

Graduates receive internationally aligned, competency-based certificates that indicate module competency level, contact hours completed, practical proficiency rating, and safety compliance status. Our certifications are IEC-compliant and recognized by utility employers across the region.

Absolutely. Stream B is specifically designed for in-service utility personnel — linesmen, technicians, and engineers. You'll need endorsement/sponsorship from your employer, prior basic electrical safety training, and a valid utility ID with signed waiver.

Entry Requirements

Find out what you need to join Power Training School programs.

Stream A (Fresh Graduates)

  • ✓ Recognized Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering or related field.
  • ✓ Foundational understanding of power systems theory.
  • ✓ Commitment to zero-tolerance safety protocols.
  • ✓ Clear background check and medical fitness for high-voltage environments.

Stream B (In-Service Utility Personnel)

  • ✓ Currently employed as a linesman, technician, or engineer at a recognized utility.
  • ✓ Endorsement / Sponsorship from employer (for CPD credits).
  • ✓ Prior completion of basic electrical safety training.
  • ✓ Valid utility ID and signed waiver.
